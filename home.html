John Williams returns in another episode in the Star Wars Universe. Just like the movies before, he fails to disappoint. Enjoy this track from the new movie Star Wars: The Force Awakens as John William takes back to a galaxy far, far, away.

Academy Award Winnner John Williams has also composed the movie soundtracks for the previous Episodes of the Star Wars Universe.

Kylo Ren Arrives at the Battle

John Williams

The Force Awakens Soundtrack | 2:01 min | 2015

We haven't seen the movie yet but judging by this particular track, we can tell Kylo Ren made a grand entrance. John Williams again helps usher in the Sith in proper fashion. Slowly and quietly he takes us on the path to battle, with a climax that screams battle.

John Williams helps sets the tempo similar to the same that he accomplished in the original trio of movies. He is the master of introducing a Sith Lord.

Imperial Attack

John Williams & London Symphony Orchestra

A New Hope Soundtrack | 6:42 min | 1977

This is the song that started it all. The first scene of the Star Wars franchise begins with an Imperial Star Destroyer attacking a small ship. We get introduced to 3 iconic characters: C3P0, R2-D2 and Princess Leia. We also learn why they are on together. But this is the best part of the scene, John Williams uses his talent to help introduce us to the Empire as well as one of the most iconic people in movies, Darth Vader.

Deal with Dark Lord

John Williams & London Symphony Orchestra

The Empire Strikes Back Soundtrack | 2:37 min | 1980

The Empire Strikes Back is considered the best episode of the Star Wars Universe. We are introduced to Lando Calrissain, a long time friend of Han Solo, and leadership of Cloud City. However, he also made a deal with Darth Vader and turned his friend over to the Empire. This track embodies that deciet while embracing the dark side. It is a stark reminder that when facing the Dark Side, nothing is sacred.

Imperial March

John Williams & London Symphony Orchestra

The Empire Strikes Back Soundtrack | 3:03 min | 1980

No matter how many times you hear this song, you remember the first time hearing it. Nothing says Empire or Darth Vader like the Imperial March. If there is one song that defines John Williams illustrious career, this would be the song. The scene associated with this song,had many of us impressed by the Empire and even wanting to seek out the Dark Side. It has become so influential that within the first 5 seconds, everyone whom hears this knows the Dark Side is coming.

The Emperor's Throne Room

John Williams & London Symphony Orchestra

Return of the Jedi Soundtrack | 3:26 min | 1983

The Emperor wanted to see if he could turn Luke to the Dark Side. He almost succeeded. His throne room is where it happened. The track helps create the sinister undertones of the Emperor's plans as he forces Luke to watch the rebels get ambushed. John Williams definitely helps establish and pace the dilemma between the light and dark side that we all face, but Luke Skywalker even more.
